# Pilot Screening Round 1 Instructions

## Purpose

Screen the first 10 PubMed records from each accepted search:

- A3 epithelium/limbus
- B3 stroma
- C4 endothelium
- D3 full-thickness/multilayer

Total pilot sample: 40 records.

## How to Screen

Use PubMed's "Best Match" order for this pilot round.

For each record, fill:

- title
- year
- journal
- PMID
- decision
- reason
- corneal_layer
- notes

## Allowed Decisions

Use only:

- Include
- Exclude
- Uncertain

## Include If

The paper appears to contain primary or clinical/translational evidence relevant to corneal biomaterials, scaffolds, hydrogels, engineered substitutes, cell sheets, decellularized matrices, biofabrication, or tissue-engineered repair/replacement.

## Exclude If

The paper is mainly:

- a review article;
- a book chapter;
- general ophthalmology without engineering content;
- disease biology without biomaterial/scaffold/cell-engineering relevance;
- pure drug delivery without regenerative scaffold or implant relevance;
- unrelated to corneal repair/replacement engineering.

## Use Uncertain If

The title/abstract suggests possible relevance but does not clearly show whether the study contains extractable engineering or biomaterial data.

## Corneal Layer Labels

Use one of:

- epithelium_limbus
- stroma
- endothelium
- full_thickness_multilayer
- multiple_layers
- unclear

## Pilot Success Criteria

The pilot criteria are acceptable if:

1. The decision rules are easy to apply.
2. Most exclusions have clear reasons.
3. At least some records from each search are includable or uncertain.
4. The searches are not dominated by reviews or irrelevant clinical disease papers.

## Next Step

After completing 40 pilot records, calculate:

- number included
- number excluded
- number uncertain
- most common exclusion reasons
- whether search rules need adjustment

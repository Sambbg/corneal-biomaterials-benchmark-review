# Pilot Title/Abstract Screening Plan

## Purpose

The PubMed pilot searches have produced a manageable but still broad evidence base. Before exporting and screening all records, a small pilot screening round will test whether the inclusion and exclusion criteria are clear.

## Accepted PubMed Pilot Searches

- A3 epithelium/limbus: 275 results
- B3 stroma: 444 results
- C4 endothelium: 417 results
- D3 full-thickness/multilayer: 182 results

Total before deduplication: 1318 records

## Pilot Screening Method

Screen the first 20 records from each accepted PubMed search using Best Match and then repeat using Most Recent if necessary.

For each record, classify as:

- Include
- Exclude
- Uncertain

## Include If

The study is likely to contain engineering or biomaterial evidence relevant to corneal repair or replacement.

## Exclude If

The record is mainly:

- a review used only for background;
- general ophthalmology;
- disease description without engineering content;
- pure drug delivery without regenerative scaffold/implant relevance;
- unrelated to corneal tissue engineering;
- lacking biomaterial, scaffold, cell, fabrication, or translational engineering relevance.

## Pilot Success Criteria

The criteria are acceptable if two conditions are met:

1. At least 30-50% of screened records from each search are relevant or possibly relevant.
2. The reasons for exclusion are clear and repeatable.

## Next Step After Pilot Screening

If the criteria work, export accepted search results from PubMed and begin deduplication.

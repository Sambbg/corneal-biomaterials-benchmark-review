# Title/Abstract Screening Rules

## Review Focus

Working review focus:

Layer-specific benchmarking of biomaterials for corneal tissue engineering, especially how materials perform against optical, mechanical, biological, and translational requirements.

## Include

Include records if the title/abstract suggests at least one of the following:

1. Corneal tissue engineering, regeneration, repair, reconstruction, or replacement.
2. Biomaterial, scaffold, hydrogel, film, membrane, decellularized matrix, bioprinting, nanofiber, or engineered construct for corneal application.
3. Cell-engineered corneal model or tissue construct with relevance to epithelial, stromal, endothelial, limbal, or full-thickness repair.
4. Experimental biomaterial performance testing relevant to corneal use, including transparency, mechanics, biocompatibility, cell adhesion, differentiation, innervation, wound healing, or transplantation.
5. Strong case-study candidate for comparison across biomaterial classes or corneal layers.

## Exclude

Exclude records if the title/abstract clearly indicates:

1. General ophthalmology without tissue engineering relevance.
2. Disease biology only, with no biomaterial, scaffold, cell engineering, regenerative construct, or repair material.
3. Drug delivery only, unless the delivery platform is also clearly a regenerative corneal scaffold or tissue-engineering construct.
4. Contact lens, surgical, diagnostic, imaging, or clinical management topic without tissue-engineering relevance.
5. General review/background article that does not provide primary biomaterial performance data.
6. Book chapter or clinical overview not suitable for evidence extraction.
7. Non-corneal tissue engineering.

## Uncertain

Use Uncertain when:

1. The title suggests possible relevance but the abstract is needed to decide.
2. The paper may involve biomaterials but corneal tissue-engineering relevance is unclear.
3. The paper appears to be about wound healing or repair biology, but scaffold/material involvement is unclear.
4. The record could be useful as background but not clearly extractable as evidence.

Do not force borderline papers into Include or Exclude too early.

## Decision Labels

Use only:

- Include
- Exclude
- Uncertain

## Exclusion Reasons

Use one of these where possible:

- Review/background only
- Book chapter/background clinical overview
- General ophthalmology/no engineering content
- Disease biology only
- Drug delivery only/no regenerative scaffold relevance
- Not corneal tissue engineering
- No biomaterial/scaffold/cell-engineering relevance
- Insufficient relevance from title/abstract

## Corneal Layer Labels

Use one of:

- epithelium_limbus
- stroma
- endothelium
- full_thickness_multilayer
- multiple_layers
- unclear

## Priority Levels

For included or uncertain records:

- High: likely core extraction/case-study candidate
- Medium: relevant but may support only part of the synthesis
- Low: background/supporting evidence only

For excluded records, leave priority blank unless there is a clear reason to keep it as background.

## Quality Control Rule

Every batch must be audited before merging into the master screening file. Borderline records should be documented in the screening notes.

# Target Journal Decision (Task #11) — Journal of Biomedical Materials Research Part B: Applied Biomaterials

## Decision

**Primary submission target: Journal of Biomedical Materials Research Part B: Applied Biomaterials (JBMR-B)**, Wiley, Q2, IF ~3.4.

Chosen over the other two Q2 shortlist options (Biomedical Materials/IOP, JTERM) for one concrete, practical reason: JBMR-B uses Wiley's **"Free Format" submission** with no disclosed hard overall word limit, which fits a data-heavy, multi-table benchmarking review (4 large tables plus 4 planned figures across ~248 corpus records) far better than a journal with a fixed page/word cap. Biomedical Materials (IOP) caps "Papers" at ~12,000 words / 14 journal pages, which is workably close but tighter, and would likely force compression of Table 2 (full comparative biomaterials table) into supplementary material. JBMR-B is also the closest topical fit — it already regularly publishes exactly this kind of biomaterial-vs-tissue-layer benchmarking work.

## Confirmed Submission Requirements (pulled 2026-07-20)

Source: Wiley JBMR-B "For Authors" page (https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/15524981/homepage/forauthors.html).

- **Submission format:** Free Format — submit as a single editable manuscript file (text, figures, tables; figures/tables may be embedded where referenced or as separate files). No mandatory template at initial submission.
- **Required sections:** Abstract, Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusions.
- **Table of contents entry:** article title, author names (corresponding author marked with asterisk), ≤80 words / 3 sentences summarizing key findings, plus one representative figure.
- **References:** any consistent style is accepted at initial submission (will be reformatted to journal style only after acceptance) — reduces upfront formatting burden.
- **ORCID:** required for at least the corresponding author (and recommended for all authors).
- **Word limit:** none explicitly published for this journal — confirmed via search; if the editorial office states one via the submission portal at the time of actual submission, that supersedes this note.

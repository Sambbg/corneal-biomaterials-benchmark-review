# Review Protocol v0.1

## Working Title

From Optical Clarity to Clinical Readiness: A Layer-Specific Benchmarking Review of Biomaterials for Corneal Tissue Engineering

## Target Journal

Primary target: Acta Biomaterialia

## Review Type

Critical narrative review with systematic search transparency.

## Core Research Question

How well do currently reported biomaterials and biofabrication approaches reproduce the optical, mechanical, biological, and translational requirements of layer-specific corneal repair or replacement?

## Central Thesis

The field of corneal tissue engineering should not evaluate biomaterials only by novelty, cytocompatibility, or isolated in vitro success. A publishable review must compare approaches against layer-specific benchmarks: transparency, biomechanics, degradation behaviour, cellular phenotype, surgical usability, safety, manufacturability, and regulatory readiness.

## Rationale

Broad reviews on corneal tissue engineering already exist. This review therefore avoids a generic "recent advances" structure and instead uses a benchmarking framework to compare biomaterials and fabrication strategies across corneal layers.

## Databases

Planned sources:

- PubMed / MEDLINE
- Scopus
- Web of Science Core Collection
- Embase
- ClinicalTrials.gov

## Search Window

Primary search window: 1 January 2018 onward.

Special attention: 2025–2026 literature, because the assignment brief requires recent studies from this period.

## Planned Search Concepts

Concept 1: cornea OR corneal  
Concept 2: tissue engineering OR bioengineering OR regenerative medicine  
Concept 3: biomaterial OR scaffold OR hydrogel OR decellularized matrix OR electrospinning OR bioprinting OR cell sheet  
Concept 4: epithelium OR limbus OR stroma OR endothelium OR full-thickness cornea  
Concept 5: transparency OR biomechanics OR phenotype OR degradation OR surgical handling OR clinical translation

## Inclusion Criteria

Include:

- Primary experimental studies
- Animal studies with quantitative corneal outcomes
- Clinical studies or first-in-human reports
- Corneal biomaterial, scaffold, hydrogel, cell sheet, decellularized matrix, electrospun scaffold, bioprinted construct, or engineered substitute studies
- Studies with extractable optical, mechanical, biological, safety, or translational data

## Exclusion Criteria

Exclude:

- Pure drug-delivery papers unless the system functions as a regenerative scaffold or implant
- General ophthalmology papers without engineering or biomaterials relevance
- Conference abstracts without enough methodological detail
- Reviews as primary evidence
- Papers without enough detail for benchmarking

## Extraction Domains

For each included primary study, extract:

- Study year
- Corneal layer target
- Study type
- Cell source
- Biomaterial or composition
- Fabrication method
- Crosslinking or surface modification
- Transparency metric
- Mechanical metric
- Degradation or swelling data
- Cell viability and phenotype markers
- In vivo or clinical model
- Surgical handling
- Immunogenicity and safety
- Clinical stage
- Main strengths
- Main limitations
- DOI

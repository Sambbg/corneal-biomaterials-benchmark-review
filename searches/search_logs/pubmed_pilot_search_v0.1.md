# PubMed Pilot Search v0.1

## Purpose

This pilot search tests whether the core PubMed search retrieves relevant corneal tissue-engineering papers for a biomaterials-led benchmarking review.

The aim is not to collect every paper yet. The aim is to check whether the search retrieves the right type of evidence: primary studies, clinical translation papers, corneal biomaterials papers, scaffold/fabrication studies, and layer-specific studies involving epithelium, stroma, endothelium, or full-thickness constructs.

## Review Title

From Optical Clarity to Clinical Readiness: A Layer-Specific Benchmarking Review of Biomaterials for Corneal Tissue Engineering

## Target Journal

Acta Biomaterialia

## Core Research Question

How well do currently reported biomaterials and biofabrication approaches reproduce the optical, mechanical, biological, and translational requirements of layer-specific corneal repair or replacement?

## Database

PubMed / MEDLINE

## Pilot Search Date

To be completed manually when the search is run.

## Pilot Search Questions

After running the search, answer:

1. How many results were retrieved?
2. Are the first 20 results mostly relevant?
3. Are key known studies retrieved?
4. Are too many irrelevant drug-delivery papers appearing?
5. Are epithelial, stromal, endothelial, and full-thickness papers represented?
6. Does the search retrieve recent 2025-2026 studies?
7. Does the search retrieve clinical or translational studies?
8. Does the search need separate layer-specific sub-searches?

## Key Known Papers to Check

The search should ideally retrieve or lead to the following evidence categories:

- Human stromal bioengineered corneal tissue studies
- Limbal epithelial cell therapy studies
- iPSC-derived corneal epithelial studies
- Corneal endothelial cell therapy studies
- Hydrogel scaffold studies
- Decellularized corneal scaffold studies
- 3D bioprinting or multi-material corneal construct studies
- Cell-sheet or engineered lamella studies
